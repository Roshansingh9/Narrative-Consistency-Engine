The Chronicles of Aldric

Chapter One: Origins

Aldric was born in the northern city of Varen in the year 1042. His father was a blacksmith of modest reputation, and his mother kept a small herb garden that supplied half the district's apothecaries. Aldric served as his father's apprentice from the age of eight, learning to shape iron and read the color of heated metal.

In the winter of 1058, a plague swept through Varen without warning. Within three months it had claimed the lives of Aldric's parents and his younger sister. Aldric, then sixteen, survived. He locked the forge, buried what remained of his family, and joined a merchant caravan heading south before the first thaw.

Chapter Two: Meros

By 1065, Aldric had established himself as a skilled swordsmith in the capital city of Meros. He rented a modest workshop near the eastern gate and was known among the city's garrison for blades that held an edge longer than any competitor's work. He was recognizable by his distinctive red cloak, which he wore year-round in memory of his mother, who had owned one like it.

Aldric never married. He refused every introduction arranged by neighbors and spoke rarely about his past. Those who knew him understood the refusal was not coldness but the result of loss — he had learned not to attach himself to things that could be taken.

Chapter Three: The Fortress

In the autumn of 1065, Aldric was arrested on charges of forging counterfeit royal seals. The evidence was circumstantial but compelling: a set of carving tools found in his workshop matched the marks on the forgeries. He maintained his innocence throughout. The court found him guilty and sentenced him to imprisonment in the Fortress of Kael.

During his three years in the Fortress, Aldric shared a cell block with Brother Celan, an elderly scholar imprisoned for distributing prohibited religious texts. Celan taught Aldric to read and write in the long hours between inspections. By the time of his release, Aldric had worked through most of the small library Celan had managed to conceal from the guards.

Chapter Four: Return

In the spring of 1068, the true forger — a seal-cutter named Davan — confessed on his deathbed to a priest, who relayed the confession to the crown. Aldric was released within the month. He returned to Meros, reclaimed his old workshop, and resumed his trade.

He lived quietly for the next twenty-three years. In 1079, he took in an orphaned girl named Mira, the daughter of a former customer, and raised her as his own. Aldric died in 1091 of a fever. Mira inherited the workshop and continued operating it for another decade.
